Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before booking a call.

Getting Started

What does the process look like?

It starts with a free 30-minute discovery call. We talk through the system or workflow in question and what you're trying to accomplish. If it's a good fit, I put together a scoped proposal with a fixed price. No surprises, no hourly billing games.

Do I need technical knowledge before we talk?

Not at all. Most clients come in knowing what's broken or what they want to accomplish — not what the technical path forward looks like. My job is to translate. You bring the business context, I bring the technical side.

What's the first step?

Book a free discovery call on the Contact page. It's 30 minutes, no obligation, and genuinely useful even if we don't end up working together.

Working Together

Do you work remotely?

Yes — primarily remote. I'm based in Kelowna, BC and work with clients across Canada. On-site engagements are available in BC for workshops and training.

Will I be dealing with junior staff or contractors?

No. I'm a solo consultant — the person you meet on the discovery call is the person who does the work. No bait-and-switch with juniors or subcontractors.

How long does a typical engagement take?

It depends on the scope. A strategy and roadmap engagement typically takes 1–2 weeks. Implementation projects range from a few days to a few weeks depending on complexity. The fractional advisor retainer is ongoing month-to-month.

Do you require long-term contracts?

No. Project engagements are fixed-scope and fixed-price. The fractional advisor retainer is month-to-month with no lock-in — cancel any time.

Legacy Modernization

How can you modernize a legacy app in days when other firms quote months?

AI-augmented development. I write comprehensive context documents and a phased plan, then direct AI agents to execute the rebuild under my review. The bottleneck stops being typing speed and becomes thinking speed. That compresses months of work into days without cutting corners on tests, CI/CD, or docs.

What legacy stacks do you handle?

Classic ASP, .NET Framework (4.x and earlier), VB6, Oracle PL/SQL packages, Crystal Reports, and legacy SQL Server systems. The modernization target is almost always .NET 8 (Blazor Server, ASP.NET Core Web API, or both) — but I'll recommend whatever fits your team.

Will this break my production system?

No. The pattern I use preserves your existing database schema, authentication (SSO/SAML/OAuth), and integrations. New system runs alongside the old until you're ready to cut over. Recent modernizations: zero breaking changes, zero downtime.

What if my codebase is huge and complex?

A 705,714-line Oracle PL/SQL package was modernized to .NET 8 Blazor in 8 days. Complexity is a scoping input, not a blocker. Start with an Assessment ($2,500) — you get a written plan with an honest timeline and cost before committing to a full rebuild.

AI & Technical

How is this different from just using ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is a tool. I help you figure out which tools are worth using, build them into your actual systems and workflows, and make sure your team knows how to use them effectively. That's the difference between a hammer and someone who builds you a house.

What AI tools and platforms do you work with?

I'm vendor-agnostic — I use whatever fits the problem. In practice, that often means Claude (Anthropic), GPT-5 (OpenAI), and purpose-built tooling layered on top of your existing systems. I don't earn commissions from any platform.

What industries do you serve?

Modernization: any organization running legacy .NET, Classic ASP, VB6, or Oracle PL/SQL — industry-agnostic. AI consulting: finance teams and small businesses. My engineering background spans higher education, e-commerce, and enterprise systems — if you have a real problem, it's worth a conversation.

Will my data be safe?

Security is core to how I build things — not an afterthought. I have deep experience with SSO, SAML, OAuth, JWT, Row Level Security, and building systems where data doesn't leak. Any solution I build will respect your data boundaries, and I'll be transparent about where data flows.

Deliverables & Knowledge Transfer

What does "knowledge transfer" mean?

It means you walk away owning the solution — not dependent on me to keep it running. Every engagement includes documentation (architecture notes, how-to guides, runbooks) and a handoff session where I walk your team through what was built and how to maintain it.

Do I own the code?

Yes. Everything built for you is yours. I retain no rights to IP developed during your engagement.

What if something goes wrong after the project ends?

Implementation projects include 30 days of post-launch support. Beyond that, I'm available for follow-on work or retainer support. I'm not the type to build something and disappear.
