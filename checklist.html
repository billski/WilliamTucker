AI Readiness Checklist

10 questions that reveal exactly where AI will help your business — and where it won't.

Most businesses that "try AI" and fail do so for one reason: they picked a tool before they understood the problem. This checklist is designed to help you find the problems first.

Go through each question honestly. Every "yes" is a signal that AI can likely help — and a starting point for a conversation about what that actually looks like in practice.

1

Do you (or your staff) spend more than 2 hours a week on a task that follows the same steps every time?

Data entry, report generation, copying information between systems, formatting documents — if it's repetitive and rule-based, it's almost always automatable. This is where AI delivers the fastest, clearest ROI.

2

Do you answer the same customer questions over and over?

Pricing, hours, policies, how your service works — if you're fielding the same questions by phone, email, or chat, an AI assistant can handle these 24/7 with consistent, accurate answers. You spend your time on the questions that actually need you.

3

Do you write similar documents from scratch each time — quotes, proposals, reports, emails?

If you're staring at a blank page when writing a proposal or weekly report, AI can generate a complete, professional first draft in under two minutes. You review and customize — you don't start from zero.

4

Do leads or follow-ups sometimes fall through the cracks because of slow response time?

Speed matters more than most businesses realize — a lead that doesn't hear back within a few hours often moves on. Automated follow-up sequences triggered by a form submission or inquiry can respond immediately, every time, without you lifting a finger.

5

Is there knowledge in your business that lives in your head — and nowhere else?

Every business has undocumented expertise: how to handle a difficult client situation, what questions to ask during intake, how the pricing works in edge cases. When that knowledge only exists in one person's head, it creates a bottleneck — and a risk. AI can help capture and surface it.

6

Do new staff ask the same questions repeatedly during onboarding?

Training new people is expensive and time-consuming. An AI knowledge base built from your existing documents, processes, and FAQs can answer routine questions instantly — freeing you and your senior staff to focus on the things that actually need human judgement.

7

Do you spend time every week building reports or summaries manually?

Weekly sales reports, monthly performance summaries, end-of-day recaps — if someone is spending time pulling numbers together and formatting them, that process can almost always be automated. Numbers in your inbox every Monday, no manual work required.

8

Do you struggle to respond to reviews, inquiries, or social comments in a timely way?

A Google review goes unanswered for a week. An inquiry sits in the inbox. These are reputation and revenue problems. AI can draft responses the moment something arrives — you approve and send in 30 seconds instead of spending 10 minutes writing from scratch.

9

Is your data scattered across spreadsheets, emails, and systems that don't talk to each other?

If getting a simple answer requires opening three different tools and copying numbers manually, you have an integration problem. AI-powered automation can connect these systems — enter data once, and everything updates. This is one of the highest-leverage fixes for small businesses.

10

Are you spending owner-level time on tasks that don't require owner-level judgement?

Scheduling, data entry, invoice follow-up, answering basic questions — these are the tasks that quietly consume 10–15 hours a week. That's time that should be spent on strategy, relationships, and growth. AI doesn't replace you; it gives back the hours that shouldn't be yours in the first place.

What your answers mean

1–3 yes

You have at least one clear AI opportunity. Start there — one well-implemented solution beats ten half-built ones. The right first project builds confidence and delivers a return you can point to.

4–6 yes

You have meaningful untapped potential. A prioritized roadmap would help you tackle the highest-ROI items first rather than trying to fix everything at once and running out of steam.

7–10 yes

AI could genuinely transform how your business runs. You're leaving significant time and money on the table — and a structured engagement would pay for itself quickly. This is worth a conversation.
